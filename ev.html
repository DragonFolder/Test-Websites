1. The Battery: Storing the Fuel

The battery pack is a massive array of lithium-ion cells located at the base of the car. It stores energy as Direct Current (DC) electricity when charged. When you press the accelerator, this energy is sent to an inverter, which converts it into Alternating Current (AC) to power the motor.

Fun Fact: When you lift your foot off the pedal, the motor reverses its role and acts as a generator. It converts motion back into electricity to recharge the battery—this is called regenerative braking.
